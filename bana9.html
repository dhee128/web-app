Banana Green (Aratikaya Kura)

Aratikaya Kura, also known as Banana Green Curry, is a traditional South Indian dish made with raw bananas. It’s a savory and flavorful curry that pairs well with rice or roti. This dish is rich in taste and offers a great way to use green bananas in a hearty and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 raw bananas (green bananas), peeled and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon oil (vegetable or coconut)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon fennel seeds (optional)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Method

  1. Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.
  2. Add fennel seeds if using, then add the chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions are translucent.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and ginger. Cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped tomato and cook until it softens.
  5. Mix in the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Stir well.
  6. Add the sliced raw bananas to the pan and stir to coat them with the spices.
  7. Pour in the water and add salt to taste. Mix well.
  8. Cover the pan and cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until the raw bananas are tender and cooked through. Stir occasionally.
  9. Once the bananas are cooked, garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with rice or roti.
