Fruit Pie

This Fruit Pie is a delightful dessert that showcases a mix of seasonal fruits encased in a buttery, flaky crust. Perfect for any occasion, it's a crowd-pleaser that highlights the natural sweetness and flavors of the fruit.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup ice water (more if needed)
  • 4 cups mixed fresh fruit (such as apples, berries, peaches, or pears), peeled and sliced if necessary
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Granulated sugar (for sprinkling on top)

Method

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour and sugar. Add cold butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough just comes together. Divide the dough into two discs, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  3. In a large bowl, combine fruit, sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss to coat the fruit evenly.
  4. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. On a floured surface, roll out one disc of dough to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Place the rolled-out dough into the pie plate, trimming any excess.
  6. Fill with the fruit mixture and dot with small pieces of butter.
  7. Roll out the second disc of dough and place over the fruit filling. Trim and crimp the edges to seal. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  8. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg and sprinkle with granulated sugar.
  9. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  10. Let the pie cool before serving to allow the filling to set.
