Apple Crumble

Apple Crumble is a classic dessert featuring tender apples topped with a buttery, crunchy crumble. It's easy to make and perfect with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Ingredients

    For the Filling:

  • 4 large apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp), peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour

    • For the Crumble Topping:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x9-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and flour. Mix well and transfer to the prepared baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, make the crumble topping by combining flour, granulated sugar, brown sugar, and cold cubed butter. Use your fingers or a pastry cutter to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in the rolled oats.
  4. Evenly sprinkle the crumble topping over the apple mixture in the baking dish.
  5. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  6. Let cool slightly before serving. Serve warm, ideally with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.
