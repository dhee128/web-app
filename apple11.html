Easy Apple Strudel

This Easy Apple Strudel is a simple and delicious pastry that combines tender apples with a flaky crust. Perfect for a quick dessert or a special treat, it’s a classic that’s sure to please everyone.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of store-bought phyllo dough (about 16 oz), thawed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 medium apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)
  • Vanilla ice cream or whipped cream (optional)

Method

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, lemon juice, raisins, and nuts (if using). Toss until the apples are well coated.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Place one sheet of phyllo dough on a clean surface and brush it lightly with melted butter. Place another sheet of phyllo on top and brush with butter. Repeat this process with 5-6 sheets of phyllo.
  4. Spoon the apple filling down the center of the phyllo dough, leaving about 2 inches on each side. Carefully fold the sides of the phyllo over the filling and then roll it up from one end to the other, creating a log shape.
  5. Transfer the strudel to the prepared baking sheet and brush the top with the remaining melted butter.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the strudel is golden brown and crispy.
  7. Allow the strudel to cool slightly before dusting with powdered sugar and serving with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream, if desired.
